Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy describes how Krubles, Inc. ("Krubles", "we", "us", or "our") collects, uses, shares, and protects personal information when you use our IT asset management platform (the "Service"). We are committed to protecting your privacy and maintaining the security of your data.

IMPORTANT: By using the Service, you consent to the collection and use of your information as described in this Privacy Policy. If you do not agree with our practices, please do not use the Service.

1. Information We Collect

1.1 Information You Provide Directly

We collect information you provide when you:

  • Create an Account: Name, email address, password, company name, job title, phone number
  • Use the Service: IT asset data (hardware/software details, serial numbers, purchase information, maintenance records)
  • Contact Support: Support inquiries, feedback, and communications
  • Make Payments: Billing information processed through Stripe (we do not store credit card numbers)
  • Configure Integrations: OAuth tokens for third-party services (Slack, Google, Microsoft)

1.2 Information Collected Automatically

When you use the Service, we automatically collect:

  • Usage Data: Pages viewed, features used, time spent, click patterns
  • Device Information: IP address, browser type, operating system, device identifiers
  • Log Data: Access times, error logs, API calls, performance metrics
  • Cookies and Tracking: Session cookies, analytics cookies, preference cookies

1.3 Information from Third Parties

We receive information from integrated third-party services:

  • OAuth Providers: Name, email, profile information (Google, Microsoft)
  • Slack: Workspace information, user IDs for notifications
  • Stripe: Payment status, transaction details, billing information

2. How We Use Your Information

2.1 Primary Purposes

We use your information to:

  • Provide the Service: Create accounts, store asset data, enable features
  • Process Payments: Handle subscriptions, billing, and invoicing
  • Communicate: Send account notifications, service updates, support responses
  • Improve the Service: Analyze usage patterns, fix bugs, develop new features
  • Ensure Security: Detect fraud, prevent abuse, protect against threats
  • Comply with Law: Meet legal obligations and respond to legal requests

2.2 Legal Basis for Processing (GDPR)

For users in the European Economic Area (EEA), we process personal data based on:

  • Contract Performance: To provide the Service you've subscribed to
  • Consent: For optional features like marketing emails and analytics
  • Legitimate Interests: For product improvement, security, and fraud prevention
  • Legal Obligation: To comply with applicable laws and regulations

2.3 Marketing Communications

We may send you:

You can unsubscribe from marketing emails at any time by clicking the unsubscribe link or updating your preferences in your account settings.

4. Data Security Measures

4.1 Technical Safeguards

We implement industry-standard security measures to protect your data:

  • Encryption: AES-256 encryption for data at rest; TLS 1.3 for data in transit
  • Access Controls: Role-based access control (RBAC) with principle of least privilege
  • Authentication: BCrypt password hashing; optional two-factor authentication (2FA)
  • Network Security: Firewalls, intrusion detection systems, DDoS protection
  • Multi-Tenant Isolation: Complete data segregation between customer organizations
  • Secure Development: Code reviews, vulnerability scanning, penetration testing

4.2 Organizational Safeguards

  • Employee Training: Regular security and privacy training for all employees
  • Access Restrictions: Limited employee access to customer data on a need-to-know basis
  • Background Checks: Pre-employment screening for employees with data access
  • Incident Response: Documented procedures for security incident handling
  • Compliance Audits: Regular security assessments and compliance reviews

4.3 Certifications and Compliance

  • SOC 2 Type II compliance (in progress)
  • GDPR compliance for European users
  • CCPA compliance for California residents
  • ISO 27001 certification (planned)

4.4 Data Breach Notification

In the event of a data breach affecting personal information, we will:

  • Notify affected users within 72 hours of discovery
  • Notify relevant regulatory authorities as required by law
  • Provide details about the breach and remediation steps
  • Offer identity protection services if appropriate

5.3 Data Deletion Requests

You may request immediate deletion of your data by contacting privacy@krubles.com. We will comply within 30 days, except where retention is required by law.

6. Your Privacy Rights

6.1 GDPR Rights (European Users)

If you are located in the EEA, UK, or Switzerland, you have the following rights under GDPR:

  • Right to Access: Request a copy of your personal data
  • Right to Rectification: Correct inaccurate or incomplete data
  • Right to Erasure ("Right to be Forgotten"): Request deletion of your data
  • Right to Restriction: Limit how we process your data
  • Right to Data Portability: Receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format
  • Right to Object: Object to processing based on legitimate interests
  • Right to Withdraw Consent: Withdraw consent at any time (does not affect prior processing)
  • Right to Lodge a Complaint: File a complaint with your local data protection authority

Response Time: We will respond to GDPR requests within 30 days.

Contact: Email our Data Protection Officer at dpo@krubles.com

6.2 CCPA Rights (California Residents)

California residents have the following rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA):

  • Right to Know: Request information about data collected, used, and shared (up to 12 months)
  • Right to Delete: Request deletion of personal information
  • Right to Opt-Out: Opt-out of the sale of personal information (we do not sell personal data)
  • Right to Non-Discrimination: Not receive discriminatory treatment for exercising your rights

Categories of Personal Information Collected:

  • Identifiers (name, email, IP address)
  • Commercial information (subscription details, payment history)
  • Internet activity (usage data, log files)
  • Professional information (job title, company name)

We do NOT sell your personal information.

Response Time: We will respond to CCPA requests within 45 days.

Contact: Email privacy@krubles.com with "California Privacy Rights" in the subject line

6.3 How to Exercise Your Rights

To exercise any of your privacy rights:

  1. Email privacy@krubles.com with your request
  2. Provide your name, email address, and account details for verification
  3. Specify which rights you wish to exercise
  4. We will verify your identity and respond within the applicable timeframe

You may also access and update many types of information directly through your account settings.
